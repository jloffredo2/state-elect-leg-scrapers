15     Section 1.  Section 1302.1 of the act of June 3, 1937
16  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code,
17  amended December 11, 1968 (P.L.1183, No.375), is amended to
18  read:
19     Section 1302.1.  Date of Application for Absentee Ballot.--
20     (a)  Applications for absentee ballots unless otherwise
21  specified shall be received in the office of the county board of
22  elections not earlier than fifty (50) days before the primary or
23  election and not later than five o'clock P.M. of the first
1  Tuesday prior to the day of any primary or election: Provided,
2  however, That in the event any elector otherwise qualified who
3  is so physically disabled or ill on or before the first Tuesday
4  prior to any primary or election that he is unable to file his
5  application or who becomes physically disabled or ill after the
6  first Tuesday prior to any primary or election and is unable to
7  appear at his polling place or any elector otherwise qualified
8  who because of the conduct of his business, duties or occupation
9  will necessarily be absent from the State or county of his
10  residence on the day of the primary or election, which fact was
11  not and could not reasonably be known to said elector on or
12  before the first Tuesday prior to any primary or election, shall
13  be entitled to an absentee ballot at any time prior to [five
14  o'clock P.M. on the first Friday] twelve o'clock noon on the
15  first Monday immediately preceding any primary or election upon
16  execution of an Emergency Application in such form prescribed by
17  the Secretary of the Commonwealth.
18     (b)  In the case of an elector who is physically disabled or
19  ill on or before the first Tuesday prior to a primary or
20  election or becomes physically disabled or ill after the first
21  Tuesday prior to a primary or election, such Emergency
22  Application shall contain a supporting affidavit from his
23  attending physician stating that due to physical disability or
24  illness said elector was unable to apply for an absentee ballot
25  on or before the first Tuesday prior to the primary or election
26  or became physically disabled or ill after that period.
27     (c)  In the case of an elector who is necessarily absent
28  because of the conduct of his business, duties or occupation
29  under the unforeseen circumstances specified in this subsection,
30  such Emergency Application shall contain a supporting affidavit
1  from such elector stating that because of the conduct of his
2  business, duties or occupation said elector will necessarily be
3  absent from the State or county of his residence on the day of
4  the primary or election which fact was not and could not
5  reasonably be known to said elector on or before the first
6  Tuesday prior to the primary or election.
7     Section 2.  This act shall take effect immediately.
