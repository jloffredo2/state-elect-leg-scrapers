19     Section 1302.3.  Absentee Electors Files and Lists.--(a)  The
20  county board of elections shall maintain at its office a file
21  containing the duplicate absentee voter's temporary registration
22  cards of every registered elector to whom an absentee ballot has
1  been sent. Such duplicate absentee voter's temporary
2  registration cards shall be filed by election districts and
3  within each election district in exact alphabetical order and
4  indexed. The registration cards so filed shall constitute the
5  Registered Absentee Voters File for the Primary or Election of
6  (date of primary or election) and shall be kept on file for a
7  period commencing the Tuesday prior to the day of the primary or
8  election until the day following the primary or election or the
9  day the county board of elections certifies the returns of the
10  primary or election, whichever date is later. Such file shall be
11  open to public inspection at all times subject to reasonable
12  safeguards, rules and regulations.
13     (b)  The county board of elections shall post in a
14  conspicuous public place at its office a master list arranged in
15  alphabetical order by election districts setting forth the name
16  and residence, and at primaries, the party enrollment, of [(1)
17  every military elector to whom an absentee ballot is being sent,
18  each such name to be prefixed with an "M";] (2) every bedridden
19  or hospitalized veteran outside the county of his residence who
20  is not registered and to whom an absentee ballot is being sent,
21  each such name to be prefixed with a "V"; and (3) every
22  registered elector who has filed his application for an absentee
23  ballot too late for the extraction of his original registration
24  card and to whom a ballot is being sent and every qualified
25  elector who has filed his application for an absentee ballot and
26  is entitled, under provisions of [the Permanent Registration Law
27  as now or hereinafter enacted by the General Assembly] law, to
28  absentee registration prior to or concurrently with the time of
29  voting, each such name to be prefixed with a "C." This list
30  shall be known as the Military, Veterans and Emergency Civilians
1  Absentee Voters File for the Primary or Election of (date of
2  primary or election) and shall be posted for a period commencing
3  the Tuesday prior to the day of the primary or election until
4  the eighth day following the primary or election or the day on
5  which the county board of elections certifies the returns of the
6  primary or election, whichever date is later. Such file shall be
7  open to public inspection at all times subject to reasonable
8  safeguards, rules and regulations. [This] Except as provided in
9  subsection (b.1), this posted list shall not contain any
10  military address or references to any military organization.
11  Upon written request, the county board shall furnish a copy of
12  such list to any candidate or party county chairman.
13     (b.1)  The county board shall also maintain a list of all
14  absentee ballots which are received from electors qualified in
15  accordance with section 1301(a), (b), (c) and (d) after five
16  o'clock P. M. on the Friday before the election but before eight
17  o'clock P. M. on the day of the election, and those received up
18  to the seventh day following the election and which contain
19  postmarks dated no later than the day preceding the election.
20  This list shall be available for public inspection upon written
21  request at the office of the county board of elections subject
22  to reasonable safeguards, rules and regulations. Upon written
23  request, the county board shall furnish a copy of such list to
24  any candidate or party county chairman.
25     (c)  Not less than five days preceding the election, the
26  chief clerk shall prepare a list for each election district
27  showing the names and post office addresses of all voting
28  residents thereof to whom official absentee ballots shall have
29  been issued. Each such list shall be prepared in duplicate,
30  shall be headed "Persons in (give identity of election district)
1  to whom absentee ballots have been issued for the election of
2  (date of election)," and shall be signed by him not less than
3  four days preceding the election. [He] Except as provided in
4  subsection (b.1), he shall post the original of each such list
5  in a conspicuous place in the office of the county election
6  board and see that it is kept so posted until the close of the
7  polls on election day subject to reasonable safeguards, rules
8  and regulations. He shall cause the duplicate of each such list
9  to be delivered to the judge of election in the election
10  district in the same manner and at the same time as are provided
11  in this act for the delivery of other election supplies, and it
12  shall be the duty of such judge of election to post such
13  duplicate list in a conspicuous place within the polling place
14  of his district and see that it is kept so posted throughout the
15  time that the polls are open. Upon written request, he shall
16  furnish a copy of such list to any candidate or party county
17  chairman.
18     Section 2.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
