15     Section 1.  Section 1302.3 of the act of June 3, 1937
16  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code,
17  amended December 11, 1968 (P.L.1183, No.375), is amended to
18  read:
19     Section 1302.3.  Absentee Electors Files and Lists.--(a) The
20  county board of elections shall maintain at its office a file
21  containing the duplicate absentee voter's temporary registration
1  cards of every registered elector to whom an absentee ballot has
2  been sent. Such duplicate absentee voter's temporary
3  registration cards shall be filed by election districts and
4  within each election district in exact alphabetical order and
5  indexed. The registration cards so filed shall constitute the
6  Registered Absentee Voters File for the Primary or Election of
7  (date of primary or election) and shall be kept on file for a
8  period commencing the Tuesday prior to the day of the primary or
9  election until the day following the primary or election or the
10  day the county board of elections certifies the returns of the
11  primary or election, whichever date is later. Such file shall be
12  open to public inspection at all times subject to reasonable
13  safeguards, rules and regulations, including requiring a written
14  request, with the identity and address of requestor given and
15  the reason for the request.
16     (b) The county board of elections shall [post in a
17  conspicuous public place] maintain at its office a master list
18  arranged in alphabetical order by election districts setting
19  forth the name and residence, and at primaries, the party
20  enrollment, of (1) every military elector to whom an absentee
21  ballot is being sent, each such name to be prefixed with an "M";
22  (2) every bedridden or hospitalized veteran outside the county
23  of his residence who is not registered and to whom an absentee
24  ballot is being sent, each such name to be prefixed with a "V";
25  and (3) every registered elector who has filed his application
26  for an absentee ballot too late for the extraction of his
27  original registration card and to whom a ballot is being sent
28  and every qualified elector who has filed his application for an
29  absentee ballot and is entitled, under provisions of the
30  Permanent Registration Law as now or hereinafter enacted by the
1  General Assembly, to absentee registration prior to or
2  concurrently with the time of voting, each such name to be
3  prefixed with a "C." This list shall be known as the Military,
4  Veterans and Emergency Civilians Absentee Voters File for the
5  Primary or Election of (date of primary or election) and shall
6  be [posted] maintained for a period commencing the Tuesday prior
7  to the day of the primary or election until the day following
8  the primary or election or the day on which the county board of
9  elections certifies the returns of the primary or election,
10  whichever date is later. Such file shall be open to public
11  inspection at all times subject to reasonable safeguards, rules
12  and regulations, including requiring a written request, with the
13  identity and address of requestor given and the reason for the
14  request. This [posted] list shall not contain any military
15  address or references to any military organization. Upon written
16  request, the county board shall furnish a copy of such list to
17  any candidate or party county chairman.
18     (c) Not less than five days preceding the election, the chief
19  clerk shall prepare a list for each election district showing
20  the names and post office addresses of all voting residents
21  thereof to whom official absentee ballots shall have been
22  issued. Each such list shall be prepared in duplicate, shall be
23  headed "Persons in (give identity of election district) to whom
24  absentee ballots have been issued for the election of (date of
25  election)," and shall be signed by him not less than four days
26  preceding the election. He shall [post the original of each such
27  list in a conspicuous place] maintain the original of each list
28  in the office of the county election board and see that it is
29  kept [so posted] until the close of the polls on election day.
30  He shall cause the duplicate of each such list to be delivered
1  to the judge of election in the election district in the same
2  manner and at the same time as are provided in this act for the
3  delivery of other election supplies, and it shall be the duty of
4  such judge of election to [post] maintain such duplicate list
5  [in a conspicuous place] within the polling place of his
6  district and see that it is kept [so posted] throughout the time
7  that the polls are open. Upon written request, he shall furnish
8  a copy of such list to any candidate [or], party county chairman
9  or other person only after securing the identity and address of
10  the person making the request and ascertaining the purpose of
11  such request.
12     Section 2.  This act shall take effect immediately.
