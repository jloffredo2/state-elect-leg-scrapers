14     Section 1.  The act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known
15  as the Pennsylvania Election Code, is amended by adding a
16  section to read:
17     Section 302.1.  Non-binding Advisory Questions.--A county
18  board of elections may cause a non-binding advisory question to
19  be placed on the ballot whenever the governing body of a
20  municipality or school district, which proposes to make an
21  expenditure the total cost of which is equal to or greater than
1  an amount equal to ten (10%) per centum of the municipality's or
2  school district's operating budget for the immediately preceding
3  fiscal year, adopts, by majority vote, a resolution asking the
4  county board to place on the ballot a question to determine the
5  will of the electorate of the municipality or school district
6  with respect to said expenditure. The governing body of the
7  municipality or school district shall file a copy of the
8  resolution with the county board. Any such question placed on
9  the ballot shall be submitted to the electorate at the next
10  general, municipal or primary election occurring more than
11  thirty days after the date of the filing of the resolution.
12     Section 2.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
