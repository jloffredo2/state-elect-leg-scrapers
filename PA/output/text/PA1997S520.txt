15     Section 1.  The act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known
16  as the Pennsylvania Election Code, is amended by adding a
17  section to read:
18     Section 526.1.  Polling Places at Retirement Communities.--
19  (a)  Notwithstanding any other provision of this act, the county
20  board of elections may select and fix at minimum one polling
21  place within each election district located within a retirement
22  community.
1     (b)  When used in this section, the words "retirement
2  community" shall mean an organized community in which a group of
3  fifty or more senior citizens live and in which services,
4  facilities, mail, meals, health services and other amenities are
5  provided through a centralized administration.
6     Section 2.  Section 527(a) of the act, amended July 1, 1987
7  (P.L.178, No.20), is amended to read:
8     Section 527.  Public Buildings to Be Used Where Possible;
9  Portable Polling Places.--(a)  [In] Except as provided in
10  section 526.1, in selecting polling places, the county board of
11  elections shall, wherever possible and practicable, select
12  schoolhouses, municipal buildings or rooms, or other public
13  buildings for that purpose. Any board of public education or
14  school directors, or county or the municipal authorities shall,
15  upon request of the county board, make arrangements for the use
16  of school property, or of county or municipal property for
17  polling places.
18     In the event no available public building as contemplated
19  under this section is situated within the boundaries of any
20  election district, the county board of elections may, not less
21  than ten days prior to any election, designate as the polling
22  place for such election district any such public building
23  situated in another election district within the same ward, or,
24  if there are no wards, then within the same borough or township
25  as the case may be, provided such other building is located in
26  an election district which is immediately adjacent to the
27  boundary of the election district for which it is to be the
28  polling place and is directly accessible therefrom by public
29  street or thoroughfare. Two or more polling places may be
30  located in the same public building under this section. A
1  polling place may be selected and designated hereunder less than
2  ten days prior to any election, with the approval of a court of
3  competent jurisdiction.
4     * * *
5     Section 3.  This act shall apply to elections occurring after
6  July 1, 1997.
7     Section 4.  This act shall take effect immediately.
