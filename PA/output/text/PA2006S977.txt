16     Section 1.  Section 1112-A(a) of the act of June 3, 1937
17  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, is
18  amended by adding a paragraph to read:
19     Section 1112-A.  Election Day Procedures and the Process of
20  Voting.--(a)  In an election district which uses an electronic
21  voting system in which votes are registered electronically, the
22  following procedures will be applicable for the conduct of the
23  election at the election district:
1     * * *
2     (4.1)  The voting system, pursuant to section 1112.1-A, shall
3  produce or require the use of an individual voter-verified paper
4  record of the voter's vote that shall be made available for
5  inspection and verification by the voter before the voter's vote
6  is cast.
7     * * *
8     Section 2.  The act is amended by adding a section to read:
9     Section 1112.1-A.  Voter-verified Record.--(a)  Voting
10  systems required to produce an individual voter-verified paper
11  record under section 1112-A(a)(4.1) shall do so pursuant to this
12  section.
13     (b)  A voter-verified paper record may include any of the
14  following:
15     (1)  A paper ballot prepared by the voter for the purpose of
16  being read by an optical scanner.
17     (2)  A paper ballot prepared by the voter to be mailed to an
18  election official, whether from a domestic or overseas location.
19     (3)  A paper ballot created through the use of a ballot
20  marking device.
21     (4)  A paper printout of the voter's vote produced by a touch
22  screen or other electronic voting machine if, in each case, the
23  record permits the voter to verify the record in accordance with
24  this section.
25     (c)  The voting system shall do all of the following:
26     (1)  Provide the voter with an opportunity to correct any
27  error made by the system in the voter-verified paper record
28  before the permanent voter-verified paper record is preserved in
29  accordance with this section.
30     (2)  Not preserve the voter-verifiable paper records in any
1  manner that makes it possible to associate a voter with the
2  record of the voter's vote.
3     (d)  The permanent voter-verified paper record produced in
4  accordance with this section shall be preserved as follows:
5     (1)  In the case of votes cast at the polling place on the
6  date of the election, within the polling place in the manner or
7  method in which all other paper ballots are preserved within
8  such polling place.
9     (2)  In the case of votes cast at the polling place prior to
10  the date of the election or cast by mail in a manner which is
11  consistent with the manner employed by the jurisdiction for
12  preserving such ballots in general.
13     (3)  In the absence of either manner or method listed in
14  clauses (1) and (2), in a manner which is consistent with the
15  manner employed by the jurisdiction for preserving paper ballots
16  in general.
17     (e)  Each permanent paper record produced shall be suitable
18  for a mandatory manual audit pursuant to subsection (f). In the
19  event of any inconsistencies or irregularities between any
20  electronic records and the individual permanent paper records,
21  the individual permanent paper records shall be the true and
22  correct record of the votes cast.
23     (f)  (1)  Each county shall conduct mandatory hand counts of
24  the voter-verified paper records in at least five (5) per centum
25  of the precincts.
26     (2)  (i)  As soon as practical following the closing of the
27  polls, the county board of elections shall do all of the
28  following:
29     (A)  Complete the initial vote count for every precinct
30  participating in the election in that county and publicly
1  announce the results of each initial vote count as soon as the
2  results are available.
3     (B)  Publicly conduct a random drawing as specified in clause
4  (3) to determine which of the precincts in the county will be
5  selected for the mandatory hand counts of the voter-verified
6  paper records as specified in clause (1). A drawing shall not
7  occur until such time as all initial vote counts have been
8  completed and the results publicly announced, but no later than
9  forty-eight hours after that time.
10     (C)  Publicly announce the date, time and location of the
11  public random drawing at least twenty-four hours before the
12  drawing is conducted.
13     (D)  Publicly announce the date, time and location of the
14  public mandatory hand counts at least twenty-four hours before
15  the hand counts are initiated.
16     (E)  Initiate mandatory hand counts of the voter-verified
17  paper records in the precincts selected as specified in
18  paragraph (B) no later than forty-eight hours after the
19  selection of precincts is made.
20     (F)  Complete hand counts no later than twenty-four hours
21  after the hand counts are initiated and publicly announce the
22  results of each mandatory hand count as soon as the results are
23  available.
24     (ii)  With respect to votes cast at the precinct or
25  equivalent location on or before the date of the election, other
26  than provisional ballots, the county board of elections shall
27  require to be counted by hand the voter-verified paper records
28  and compare those records with the initial count of the votes as
29  announced by the precinct.
30     (iii)  With respect to votes cast other than at the precinct
1  on the date of the election or votes cast by provisional ballot
2  on the date of the election which are certified and counted by
3  the county on or after the date of the election, including votes
4  cast by absent uniformed services voters and overseas voters,
5  the county board of elections shall count by hand the applicable
6  voter-verified paper records and compare its count with the
7  machine tally of those votes.
8     (3)  The selection of the precincts in a county in which the
9  county board of elections shall conduct hand counts of the
10  voter-verified paper records as specified in this subsection
11  shall be made publicly by the county board of elections on an
12  entirely random basis using a uniform distribution in which all
13  precincts in a county have an equal chance of being selected.
14  The timing of when the random selection is made shall be
15  specified in clause (2)(i).
16     (4)  If the county board of elections finds that any of the
17  hand counts conducted under this subsection show a discrepancy
18  between the hand count and the initial vote count in a
19  jurisdiction within the county, the county board of elections
20  shall conduct hand counts under this subsection at such
21  additional precincts within the county as the county board of
22  elections considers appropriate to resolve any concerns and
23  ensure the accuracy of the results.
24     (5)  (i)  As soon as practicable after the completion of an
25  audit conducted under this subsection, the county board of
26  elections shall announce and publish the results of the audit
27  and shall include in the announcement a comparison of the
28  results of the election in the precinct as determined by the
29  county board of elections under the audit and the initial vote
30  count in the precinct as announced by the county board of
1  elections as specified in clause (2)(i)(A), broken down by the
2  categories of votes described in clause (2)(ii) and (iii). Any
3  discrepancies between the corresponding audit results and
4  initial vote counts shall be duly noted, along with a
5  description of the actions taken by the county board of
6  elections for resolution of the discrepancies.
7     (ii)  No county may certify the results of any election which
8  is subject to an audit under this subsection prior to the
9  completion of the audit and the announcement and publication of
10  the results of the audit under subclause (i).
11     Section 3.  The addition of sections 1112-A(a)(4.1) and
12  1112.1-A shall apply to elections occurring on or one year from
13  the effective date of this section.
14     Section 4.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
