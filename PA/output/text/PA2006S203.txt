18     Section 1306.  Voting by Absentee Electors.--(a) At any time
19  after receiving an official absentee ballot, but on or before
20  [five o'clock P. M. on the Friday prior to] the day of the
21  primary or election, the elector shall, in secret, proceed to
22  mark the ballot only in black lead pencil, indelible pencil or
1  blue, black or blue-black ink, in fountain pen or ball point
2  pen, and then fold the ballot, enclose and securely seal the
3  same in the envelope on which is printed, stamped or endorsed
4  "Official Absentee Ballot." This envelope shall then be placed
5  in the second one, on which is printed the form of declaration
6  of the elector, and the address of the elector's county board of
7  election and the local election district of the elector. The
8  elector shall then fill out, date and sign the declaration
9  printed on such envelope. Such envelope shall then be securely
10  sealed and the elector shall send same by mail, postage prepaid,
11  except where franked, or deliver it in person to said county
12  board of election. If the elector shall send the absentee ballot
13  by mail, the envelope containing the absentee ballot must be
14  postmarked on or before the day of the primary or election:
15     Provided, however, That any elector, spouse of the elector or
16  dependent of the elector, qualified in accordance with the
17  provisions of section 1301, subsections (e), (f), (g) and (h) to
18  vote by absentee ballot as herein provided, shall be required to
19  include on the form of declaration a supporting declaration in
20  form prescribed by the Secretary of the Commonwealth, to be
21  signed by the head of the department or chief of division or
22  bureau in which the elector is employed, setting forth the
23  identity of the elector, spouse of the elector or dependent of
24  the elector:
25     Provided further, That any elector who has filed his
26  application in accordance with section 1302 subsection (e) (2),

28  physical disability, shall be excused from signing upon making a
29  declaration which shall be witnessed by one adult person in
30  substantially the following form: I hereby declare that I am
1  unable to sign my declaration for voting my absentee ballot
2  without assistance because I am unable to write by reason of my

13     Section 2.  Section 1308(a) of the act, amended December 11,
14  1968 (P.L.1183, No.375), is amended to read:
15     Section 1308.  Canvassing of Official Absentee Ballots.--
16     (a)  The county boards of election, upon receipt of official
17  absentee ballots in such envelopes, shall safely keep the same
18  in sealed or locked containers until they distribute same to the
19  appropriate local election districts in a manner prescribed by
20  the Secretary of the Commonwealth.
21     The county board of elections shall then distribute the
22  absentee ballots, unopened, to the absentee voter's respective
23  election district concurrently with the distribution of the
24  other election supplies. Absentee ballots shall be canvassed
25  immediately and continuously without interruption until
26  completed after the close of the polls on the day of the
27  election in each election district. The results of the canvass
28  of the absentee ballots shall then be included in and returned
29  to the county board with the returns of that district. No
30  absentee ballot shall be counted which is received in the office
1  of the county board of election later than five o'clock P. M. on
2  the [Friday immediately preceding] fourteenth day after the
3  primary or November election. Notwithstanding any other
4  provision of law, if an absentee ballot is received too late to
5  include with the distribution of the election supplies but
6  before the deadline provided for by this subsection, the county
7  board shall count and include the absentee ballot with the
8  election returns.
