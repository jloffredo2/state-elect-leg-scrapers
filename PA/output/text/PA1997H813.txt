16     Section 1.  Sections 1813 and 1814 of the act of June 3, 1937
17  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, are
18  amended to read:
19     Section 1813.  False Signatures and Statements in Nomination
20  Petitions and Papers.--If any person shall knowingly make a
21  false statement in any affidavit required by the provisions of
22  this act, to be appended to or to accompany a nomination
1  petition or a nomination paper, or if any person shall
2  fraudulently sign any name not his own to any nomination
3  petition or nomination paper, or if any person shall
4  fraudulently alter any nomination petition or nomination paper
5  without the consent of the signers, he shall be guilty of a
6  misdemeanor, and, upon conviction thereof, shall be sentenced to
7  pay a fine not exceeding five hundred ($500) dollars, or to
8  undergo imprisonment of not more than one (1) year, or both, in
9  the discretion of the court. Such person shall not be permitted
10  to participate as a candidate in a public election in this
11  Commonwealth until all costs and fines resulting from violation
12  of this act have been paid in full.
13     Section 1814.  Nomination Petitions; Certificates and Papers;
14  Destruction; Fraudulent Filing; Suppression.--Any person who
15  shall falsely make any nomination certificate or who shall
16  wilfully deface or destroy any nomination petition, nomination
17  certificate or nomination paper, or any part thereof, or any
18  letter of withdrawal, or who shall file any nomination petition,
19  nomination certificate or nomination paper or letter of
20  withdrawal knowing the same, or any part thereof, to be falsely
21  made, or who shall suppress any nomination petition, nomination
22  certificate or nomination paper, or any part thereof, which has
23  been duly filed, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and, upon
24  conviction thereof, shall be sentenced to pay a fine not
25  exceeding one thousand ($1,000) dollars, or to undergo an
26  imprisonment of not more than one (1) year, or both, in the
27  discretion of the court. Such person shall not be permitted to
28  participate as a candidate in a public election in this
29  Commonwealth until all costs and fines resulting from a
30  violation of this act have been paid in full.
1     Section 2.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
