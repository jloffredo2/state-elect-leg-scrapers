15     Section 1.  The act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known
16  as the Pennsylvania Election Code, is amended by adding a
17  section to read:
18     Section 1205.1.  Accessibility of Polling Places to Voters
19  with Handicaps and Elderly Voters.--(a)  The county board of
20  elections shall assure that all polling places to be used in
21  connection with an election are accessible to voters with
1  handicaps and elderly voters and that there is an accessible
2  route leading to the polling site.
3     (b)  Subsection (a) shall not apply to a polling place:
4     (1)  In the case of an emergency which prohibits the use of
5  the accessible polling place on the day of the election, as
6  determined by the county board of elections; or
7     (2)  If the county board of elections determines that all
8  potential polling places have been surveyed and an accessible
9  place is not available and the county is not able to make one
10  temporarily accessible in the area involved and the county board
11  of elections assures that a voter with a handicap or an elderly
12  voter assigned to an inaccessible polling place, upon advance
13  request of such voter, will be assigned to an accessible polling
14  place or will be provided with alternative means of casting a
15  ballot on the day of the election.
16     (c)  The Secretary of the Commonwealth shall make available
17  registration and voting aids for all elections for individuals
18  with handicaps and elderly individuals, including:
19     (1)  Instructions, printed in large type, conspicuously
20  displayed at each registration facility and each polling place;
21  and
22     (2)  Information by telecommunications devices for the deaf.
23  The Secretary of the Commonwealth shall provide public notice,
24  calculated to reach elderly voters and voters with handicaps, of
25  the availability of these registration and voting aids not later
26  than general public notice of registration and voting is
27  provided.
28     (d)  Definitions.--As used in this section, the following
29  words and phrases shall have the meanings given to them in this
30  subsection:
1     "Accessible" shall describe a building, building site or
2  portion thereof which can be approached and entered and which
3  substantially conforms with standards set forth in 34 Pa. Code
4  §§ 47.111 through 47.131 (relating to facilities for
5  handicapped), promulgated pursuant to the act of September 1,
6  1965 (P.L.459, No.235), entitled, "An act requiring that certain
7  buildings and facilities constructed with Commonwealth funds
8  adhere to certain principles, standards and specifications to
9  make the same accessible to and usable by the physically
10  handicapped, and providing for enforcement."
11     "Accessible route" shall mean a continuous unobstructed path
12  which connects all areas within a building and a building site
13  that can be negotiated by a person with a severe physical
14  handicap using a wheelchair and that is also safe for and usable
15  by people with other physical handicaps. Interior accessibility
16  routes include, but are not limited to, corridors, floors,
17  ramps, elevators, lifts and clear floor space at voting
18  machines.
19     "Elderly" shall mean a person who resides in this
20  Commonwealth and is sixty-five (65) years of age or older.
21     "Handicap" shall mean a permanent or temporary physical
22  impairment, including an impaired sensory or manual ability,
23  which results in a functional limitation in access to and use of
24  a building or facility.
25     Section 2.  (a)  Not later than December 31 of each even-
26  numbered year, each county board of elections shall report to
27  the Secretary of the Commonwealth, in a manner to be determined
28  by the secretary, the number of accessible and inaccessible
29  polling places in the county and the reasons for each case of
30  inaccessibility.
1     (b)  Not later than April 30 of each odd-numbered year, the
2  Secretary of the Commonwealth shall compile the information
3  reported under subsection (a) and shall transmit the information
4  to the appropriate standing committees of the Senate and the
5  House of Representatives.
6     Section 3.  This act shall apply to elections occurring after
7  January 1, 1995.
8     Section 4.  This act shall take effect immediately.
