1     Section 1.  Section 201 of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333,
2  No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, is amended to
3  read:
4     Section 201.  Powers and Duties of the Secretary of the
5  Commonwealth.--The Secretary of the Commonwealth shall exercise
6  in the manner provided by this act all powers granted to him by
7  this act, and shall perform all the duties imposed upon him by
8  this act, which shall include the following:
9     (a) To determine, in accordance with the provisions of this
10  act, the forms of nomination petitions and papers, expense
11  accounts and all other forms and records, the form of which he
12  is required to determine under the provisions of this act. The
13  form of nomination petitions and papers shall require a
14  statement as to whether the candidate has ever been convicted
15  of, or pleaded guilty or no contest to, an offense graded higher
16  than a misdemeanor of the first degree.
17     (b) To examine and reexamine voting machines, and to approve
18  or disapprove them for use in this State, in accordance with the
19  provisions of this act.
20     (c) To certify to county boards of elections for primaries
21  and elections the names of the candidates for President and
22  Vice-President of the United States, presidential electors,
23  United States senators, representatives in Congress and all
24  State offices, including senators, representatives, and judges
25  of all courts of record, and delegates and alternate delegates
26  to National Conventions, and members of State committees, and
27  the form and wording of constitutional amendments or other
28  questions to be submitted to the electors of the State at large.
29     (d) To receive and determine, as hereinafter provided, the
30  sufficiency of nomination petitions, certificates and papers of
1  candidates for President of the United States, presidential
2  electors, United States senators, representatives in Congress
3  and all State offices, including senators, representatives and
4  judges of all courts of record, and delegates and alternate
5  delegates to National Conventions and members of State
6  committees.
7     (e) To receive such reports from county boards of elections
8  as are required by this act, and to demand such additional
9  reports on special matters as he may deem necessary.
10     (f) To receive from county boards of elections the returns of
11  primaries and elections, to canvass and compute the votes cast
12  for candidates and upon questions as required by the provisions
13  of this act; to proclaim the results of such primaries and
14  elections, and to issue certificates of election to the
15  successful candidates at such elections, except in cases where
16  that duty is imposed by law on another officer or board.
17     (g) To perform such other duties as may be prescribed by law.
18     SECTION 2.  SECTION 1218 OF THE ACT, AMENDED FEBRUARY 13,      <--
19  1989 (P.L.1, NO.1), IS AMENDED AND THE SECTION IS AMENDED BY
20  ADDING A SUBSECTION TO READ:
21     SECTION 1218.  ASSISTANCE IN VOTING.--
22     (A)  NO VOTER SHALL BE PERMITTED TO RECEIVE ANY ASSISTANCE IN
23  VOTING AT ANY [PRIMARY OR] ELECTION, UNLESS THERE IS RECORDED
24  UPON [HIS] THE VOTER'S REGISTRATION CARD [HIS] A DECLARATION, OR
25  UNLESS THE VOTER DECLARES IN WRITING AT THE POLLING PLACE, THAT,
26  BY REASON OF BLINDNESS, DISABILITY, OR INABILITY TO READ OR
27  WRITE, [HE] THE VOTER IS UNABLE TO READ THE NAMES ON THE BALLOT
28  OR ON THE VOTING MACHINE LABELS, OR THAT [HE] THE VOTER HAS A
29  PHYSICAL DISABILITY WHICH RENDERS [HIM] THE VOTER UNABLE TO SEE
30  OR MARK THE BALLOT OR OPERATE THE VOTING MACHINE, OR TO ENTER
1  THE VOTING COMPARTMENT OR VOTING MACHINE BOOTH WITHOUT
2  ASSISTANCE, THE EXACT NATURE OF SUCH CONDITION BEING RECORDED ON
3  SUCH REGISTRATION CARD OR DECLARATION, AND UNLESS THE ELECTION
4  OFFICERS ARE SATISFIED THAT [HE] THE VOTER STILL SUFFERS FROM
5  THE SAME CONDITION. THE DECLARATION COMPLETED IN THE POLLING
6  PLACE SHALL BE IN A FORM APPROVED BY THE SECRETARY OF THE
7  COMMONWEALTH, SUBSTANTIALLY AS FOLLOWS:
8     I, (NAME AND ADDRESS OF ELECTOR REQUIRING ASSISTANCE) BY
9     REASON OF (REASON FOR NEED OF ASSISTANCE) AM UNABLE TO VOTE
10     WITHOUT THE ASSISTANCE OF (NAME AND ADDRESS OF PERSON
11     RENDERING ASSISTANCE).
12  DATE
13  WITNESSED BY (NAME OF WITNESS)
14  (SIGNATURE OF JUDGE OF ELECTION)
15     (B)  ANY ELECTOR WHO IS ENTITLED TO RECEIVE ASSISTANCE IN
16  VOTING UNDER THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION SHALL BE PERMITTED
17  BY THE JUDGE OF ELECTION TO SELECT A PERSON OF THE ELECTOR'S
18  CHOICE TO ENTER THE VOTING COMPARTMENT OR VOTING MACHINE BOOTH
19  WITH HIM TO ASSIST HIM IN VOTING, SUCH ASSISTANCE TO BE RENDERED
20  INSIDE THE VOTING COMPARTMENT OR VOTING MACHINE BOOTH EXCEPT
21  THAT THE ELECTOR'S EMPLOYER OR AN AGENT OF THE EMPLOYER OR AN
22  OFFICER OR AGENT OF THE ELECTOR'S UNION SHALL NOT BE ELIGIBLE TO
23  ASSIST THE ELECTOR.
24     (C)  IN EVERY CASE OF ASSISTANCE UNDER THE PROVISIONS OF THIS
25  SECTION, THE JUDGE OF ELECTION SHALL FORTHWITH ENTER IN WRITING
26  IN A BOOK TO BE FURNISHED BY THE COUNTY BOARD OF ELECTIONS, TO
27  BE KNOWN AS THE RECORD OF ASSISTED VOTERS--(1) THE VOTER'S NAME;
28  (2) A STATEMENT OF THE FACTS WHICH ENTITLE HIM TO RECEIVE
29  ASSISTANCE; AND (3) THE NAME OF THE PERSON FURNISHING THE
30  ASSISTANCE. THE RECORD OF ASSISTED VOTERS SHALL BE RETURNED BY
1  THE JUDGE OF ELECTION TO THE COUNTY BOARD OF ELECTIONS WITH THE
2  OTHER PAPERS, AS HEREINAFTER PROVIDED, AND SAID COUNTY BOARD
3  SHALL PERMIT THE SAME TO BE EXAMINED ONLY UPON THE WRITTEN ORDER
4  OF A JUDGE OF THE COURT OF COMMON PLEAS: PROVIDED, HOWEVER, THAT
5  SUCH RECORD SHALL BE SUBJECT TO SUBPOENA TO THE SAME EXTENT TO
6  WHICH OTHER ELECTION RECORDS MAY BE SUBPOENAED: AND PROVIDED
7  FURTHER, THAT THE COUNTY ELECTION BOARD SHALL PERMIT ANY
8  REGISTRATION COMMISSION TO EXAMINE ANY RECORDS OF ASSISTED
9  VOTERS WITHOUT A COURT ORDER, IN ORDER THAT THE REGISTRATION
10  COMMISSION MAY ASCERTAIN WHETHER ELECTORS, WHO HAVE DECLARED, AT
11  THE TIME OF REGISTRATION, THEIR NEED FOR ASSISTANCE, ACTUALLY
12  DID RECEIVE ASSISTANCE WHEN VOTING AT ANY ELECTION.
13     (D)  SUBSEQUENT TO THE ELECTION, THE COUNTY BOARD OF
14  ELECTIONS SHALL RECORD THE FACTS OF THE DECLARATION COMPLETED IN
15  THE POLLING PLACE UNDER SUBSECTION (A) ONTO THE ELECTOR'S
16  REGISTRATION CARD, INCLUDING THE REASON THE ELECTOR REQUIRES
17  ASSISTANCE.
18     SECTION 3.  SECTION 1302.1 OF THE ACT, AMENDED DECEMBER 11,
19  1968 (P.L.1183, NO.375), IS AMENDED TO READ:
20     SECTION 1302.1.  DATE OF APPLICATION FOR ABSENTEE BALLOT.--
21     (A)  APPLICATIONS FOR ABSENTEE BALLOTS UNLESS OTHERWISE
22  SPECIFIED SHALL BE RECEIVED IN THE OFFICE OF THE COUNTY BOARD OF
23  ELECTIONS NOT EARLIER THAN FIFTY (50) DAYS BEFORE THE PRIMARY OR
24  ELECTION AND NOT LATER THAN FIVE O'CLOCK P.M. OF THE FIRST
25  TUESDAY PRIOR TO THE DAY OF ANY PRIMARY OR ELECTION: PROVIDED,
26  HOWEVER, THAT IN THE EVENT ANY ELECTOR OTHERWISE QUALIFIED WHO
27  IS SO PHYSICALLY DISABLED OR ILL ON OR BEFORE THE FIRST TUESDAY
28  PRIOR TO ANY PRIMARY OR ELECTION THAT HE IS UNABLE TO FILE HIS
29  APPLICATION OR WHO BECOMES PHYSICALLY DISABLED OR ILL AFTER THE
30  FIRST TUESDAY PRIOR TO ANY PRIMARY OR ELECTION AND IS UNABLE TO
1  APPEAR AT HIS POLLING PLACE OR ANY ELECTOR OTHERWISE QUALIFIED
2  WHO BECAUSE OF THE CONDUCT OF HIS BUSINESS, DUTIES OR OCCUPATION
3  WILL NECESSARILY BE ABSENT FROM THE STATE OR COUNTY OF HIS
4  RESIDENCE ON THE DAY OF THE PRIMARY OR ELECTION, WHICH FACT WAS
5  NOT AND COULD NOT REASONABLY BE KNOWN TO SAID ELECTOR ON OR
6  BEFORE THE FIRST TUESDAY PRIOR TO ANY PRIMARY OR ELECTION, SHALL
7  BE ENTITLED TO AN ABSENTEE BALLOT AT ANY TIME PRIOR TO [FIVE
8  O'CLOCK P.M. ON THE FIRST FRIDAY] THE CLOSE OF BUSINESS ON THE
9  FIRST MONDAY IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING ANY PRIMARY OR ELECTION UPON
10  EXECUTION OF AN EMERGENCY APPLICATION IN SUCH FORM PRESCRIBED BY
11  THE SECRETARY OF THE COMMONWEALTH.
12     (B)  IN THE CASE OF AN ELECTOR WHO IS PHYSICALLY DISABLED OR
13  ILL ON OR BEFORE THE FIRST TUESDAY PRIOR TO A PRIMARY OR
14  ELECTION OR BECOMES PHYSICALLY DISABLED OR ILL AFTER THE FIRST
15  TUESDAY PRIOR TO A PRIMARY OR ELECTION, SUCH EMERGENCY
16  APPLICATION SHALL CONTAIN A SUPPORTING AFFIDAVIT FROM HIS
17  ATTENDING PHYSICIAN STATING THAT DUE TO PHYSICAL DISABILITY OR
18  ILLNESS SAID ELECTOR WAS UNABLE TO APPLY FOR AN ABSENTEE BALLOT
19  ON OR BEFORE THE FIRST TUESDAY PRIOR TO THE PRIMARY OR ELECTION
20  OR BECAME PHYSICALLY DISABLED OR ILL AFTER THAT PERIOD.
21     (C)  IN THE CASE OF AN ELECTOR WHO IS NECESSARILY ABSENT
22  BECAUSE OF THE CONDUCT OF HIS BUSINESS, DUTIES OR OCCUPATION
23  UNDER THE UNFORESEEN CIRCUMSTANCES SPECIFIED IN THIS SUBSECTION,
24  SUCH EMERGENCY APPLICATION SHALL CONTAIN A SUPPORTING AFFIDAVIT
25  FROM SUCH ELECTOR STATING THAT BECAUSE OF THE CONDUCT OF HIS
26  BUSINESS, DUTIES OR OCCUPATION SAID ELECTOR WILL NECESSARILY BE
27  ABSENT FROM THE STATE OR COUNTY OF HIS RESIDENCE ON THE DAY OF
28  THE PRIMARY OR ELECTION WHICH FACT WAS NOT AND COULD NOT
29  REASONABLY BE KNOWN TO SAID ELECTOR ON OR BEFORE THE FIRST
30  TUESDAY PRIOR TO THE PRIMARY OR ELECTION.

5     Any candidate or political committee, authorized by a

15  candidate, chairman or treasurer of the political committee
16  within twenty-four (24) hours of receipt of the contribution. It
17  shall be the duty of the supervisor to confirm the substance of
18  such telegram [or], mailgram or facsimile. Original reports
19  submitted by facsimile shall also be sent to the appropriate
20  supervisor by regular mail within twenty-four (24) hours of
21  receipt of the contribution. Any candidate in his own behalf, or
22  chairman, treasurer or candidate in behalf of the political
23  committee may also comply with this section by appearing
24  personally before such supervisor and reporting such late
25  contributions or pledges.
