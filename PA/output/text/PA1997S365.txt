15     Section 1.  Section 909 of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333,
16  No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, amended
17  February 19, 1986 (P.L.29, No.11), is amended to read:
18     Section 909.  Petition May Consist of Several Sheets;
19  Affidavit of Circulator.--Said nomination petition may be on one
20  or more sheets, and different sheets must be used for signers
21  resident in different counties. If more than one sheet is used,
22  they shall be bound together when offered for filing if they are
1  intended to constitute one petition, and each sheet shall be
2  numbered consecutively beginning with number one, at the foot of
3  each page. In cases of petitions for delegate or alternate
4  delegate to National conventions, each sheet shall contain a
5  notation indicating the presidential candidate to whom he is
6  committed or the term "uncommitted." [Each sheet shall have
7  appended thereto] Printed upon each sheet shall be the affidavit
8  of the circulator [of each sheet], setting forth--(a) that he or
9  she is a qualified elector duly registered and enrolled as a
10  member of the designated party of the State, or of the political
11  district, as the case may be, referred to in said petition,
12  unless said petition relates to the nomination of a candidate
13  for a court of common pleas, for the Philadelphia Municipal
14  Court or for the Traffic Court of Philadelphia or for justice of
15  the peace, in which event the circulator need not be a duly
16  registered and enrolled member of the designated party; (b) his
17  residence, giving city, borough or township, with street and
18  number, if any; (c) that the signers thereto signed with full
19  knowledge of the contents of the petition; (d) that their
20  respective residences are correctly stated therein; (e) that
21  they all reside in the county named in the affidavit; (f) that
22  each signed on the date set opposite his name; and (g) that, to
23  the best of affiant's knowledge and belief, the signers are
24  qualified electors and duly registered and enrolled members of
25  the designated party of the State, or of the political district,
26  as the case may be. No affidavit may be executed on any form
27  other than that printed upon the sheet, and nothing may be
28  attached or appended to the sheet under any circumstances.
29     Section 2.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
