13     Section 1.  The definition of "registration card" in section
14  102 of the act of June 30, 1995 (P.L.170, No.25), known as the
15  Pennsylvania Voter Registration Act, is amended to read:
16  Section 102.  Definitions.
17     The following words and phrases when used in this act shall
18  have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
19  context clearly indicates otherwise:
20     * * *
21     "Registration card."  A registration record containing all
1  information required on the registration application, including
2  the elector's signature, and suitable space for the insertion by
3  the appropriate official of the following information:
4         (1)  The ward and election district of residence.
5         (1.1)  The last four digits of the elector's Social
6     Security number.
7         (2)  The registrant's street address.
8         (3)  Data required to be given upon removal from the
9     registrant's residence.
10         (4)  The date of each election at which the registrant
11     votes.
12         (5)  The number and letter of the stub of the ballot
13     issued to the registrant or the registrant's number in the
14     order of admission to the voting machines.
15         (6)  The initials of the election officer who enters the
16     record of voting in the district register.
17         (7)  Whether the registrant needs assistance to vote and,
18     if so, the nature of the disability.

20     Section 2.  Sections 301, 523(b) and 527(a)(1) of the act are
21  amended to read:
22  Section 301.  Regulations.
23     The secretary shall promulgate regulations necessary to
24  administer this act. The regulations shall include, but not be
25  limited to, provisions which do all of the following:
26         (1)  Provide for applicants to submit their voter
27     registration application to the commission, the Department of
28     Transportation and other agencies designated in section 525.
29         (2)  Prescribe a procedure for the return of completed
30     voter registration applications from the Department of
1     Transportation, the Department of Public Welfare, armed
2     forces recruitment centers, Offices of the Clerk of Orphan's
3     Court and all other offices under this act to the secretary
4     or the appropriate commission.
5         [(3)  Prescribe a procedure to conduct a study of the
6     technological needs and other aspects of the development and
7     implementation of a Statewide central registry of registered
8     qualified voters, and the study shall be completed by July 1,
9     1996.]
10         (4)  Establish a Statewide central registry of registered
11     qualified voters. To assist in establishment of the Statewide
12     central registry, the secretary may appoint a task force to
13     recommend an implementation strategy, which shall include
14     provision for making grants available to each commission.
15  Section 523.  Application with driver's license application.
16     * * *
17     (b)  Process.--
18         (1)  The Department of Transportation shall provide for
19     an application for voter registration as part of a driver's
20     license application.
21         (2)  The format of the driver's license/voter
22     registration application shall be determined and prescribed
23     by the secretary and the Secretary of Transportation. The
24     application shall contain in every case an entry that the
25     person is registered and show the person's address of record.
26         (3)  The voter registration application portion of the
27     application shall contain all the requirements of an official
28     voter registration application specified in section 527. The
29     voter registration portion of the application:
30             (i)  may not require any information that duplicates
1         information required in the driver's license portion of
2         the form, other than a second signature; and
3             (ii)  may require only the minimum amount of
4         information necessary to prevent duplicate voter
5         registration, to enable the registration commission to
6         assess the eligibility of the applicant and to administer
7         voter registration and other parts of the election
8         process.
9     * * *
10  Section 527.  Preparation and distribution of applications.
11     (a)  Form.--
12         (1)  The secretary shall prescribe the form of official
13     voter registration application. The official voter
14     registration application shall provide space for the
15     following information about the applicant:
16             (i)  Full name.
17             (i.1)  Last four digits of Social Security number.
18             (ii)  Address of residence. If the residence is a
19         portion only of the house, the location or number of the
20         room, apartment or floor which is occupied.
21             (iii)  Mailing address if different than address of
22         residence.
23             (iv)  Name and residence address on previous
24         registration and the year of that registration.
25             (v)  Designation of political party, for the purpose
26         of voting at a primary election.
27             (vi)  Date of birth.
28             (vii)  Telephone number. An application shall not be
29         rejected because of noncompliance with this subparagraph.
30             (viii)  Race. An application shall not be rejected
1         because of noncompliance with this subparagraph.
2             (ix)  Proof that the applicant has been a citizen of
3         the United States for at least one month prior to the
4         next election.

6     Section 3.  Section 702 of the act is amended by adding a
7  subsection to read:
8  Section 702.  District registers.
9     * * *
10     (b.1)  Change of address.--Where the registrant's place of
11  residence has changed, the registration card file or the
12  computer list, as the case may be, shall show both the
13  registrant's current place of residence and the registrant's
14  previous place of residence.
15     * * *
16     Section 4.  The act is amended by adding sections to read:
17  Section 909.1.  Purging of duplicate registrations.
18     In addition to the procedure established under section 909,
19  the commission may cancel or suspend a registration where it
20  appears that duplicate registrations listing the same address
21  have been issued.
22  Section 5101.1.  Federal cooperation and funds.
23     (a)  Verification of information.--The secretary shall call
24  upon the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service to
25  cooperate with each commission to verify proof of citizenship
26  and Social Security numbers submitted by applicants for
27  registration.
28     (b)  Application for Federal funds.--With the approval of the
29  Governor, the secretary shall make an application for Federal
30  funds to carry out the voter removal program under section 1901.
1     Section 5.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
