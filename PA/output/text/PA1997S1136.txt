19                               AN ACT
20  Providing for the compensation of county officers in counties of

2     district attorneys in cities and counties of the first class,
3     for compensation of district election officers IN ALL          <--

6     Section 2.  The act is amended by adding a section to read:
7     Section 10.2.  (a)  In all counties, REGARDLESS OF CLASS the   <--
8  compensation of judges of election, inspectors of election,
9  clerks and machine operators as provided in Article IV of the
10  act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the
11  "Pennsylvania Election Code," shall be fixed by the county board
12  of elections for each election in accordance with the following:
13           Election Officers              Minimum       Maximum
14                                       Compensation   Compensation
15      Judges of elections                $45             $100
16      Inspectors of Election,
17      clerks and machine operators       $40             $95
18     (b)  The county board of elections may, in its discretion,
19  establish different per diem rates within the minima and maxima
20  provided for in subsection (a) based on the number of votes cast
21  for the following groups:
22         (1)  150 votes or less.
23         (2)  151 to 300 votes.
24         (3)  301 to 500 votes.
25         (4)  501 to 750 votes.
26         (5)  751 votes and over.
27     (c)  For transmitting returns of elections and the ballot box
28  or boxes, all judges of elections shall be entitled to receive
29  the additional sum of ten dollars ($10).
30     (d)  The county board of elections may, in its discretion,
1  require the minority inspector of elections to accompany the
2  judge of elections in transmitting the returns of elections in
3  which case the minority inspector of elections shall be entitled
4  to receive the additional sum of ten dollars ($10).
5     (e)  The person furnishing transportation to the judge of
6  elections and the minority inspector in transmitting returns and
7  ballot boxes shall be entitled to a minimum of thirty cents
8  ($.30) per circular mile from the polling place to the county
9  court house. The name of such person shall appear on the voucher
10  of the judge of elections and only one person shall receive
11  mileage compensation.
12     (f)  A constable or deputy constable performing duties under
13  section 1207 of the "Pennsylvania Election Code," shall receive
14  compensation at the same rate payable to an inspector.
15     (g)  When a primary and special election or a special
16  election and a general or municipal election take place on the
17  same date, they shall be construed as one election for the
18  purpose of receiving compensation.
19     (H)  COMPENSATION AND OTHER PAYMENTS RECEIVED BY ELECTION      <--
20  OFFICIALS PURSUANT TO THIS SECTION SHALL NOT BE DEEMED INCOME
21  CLASSIFIED AND CATEGORIZED UNDER SECTION 303 OF THE ACT OF MARCH
22  4, 1971 (P.L.6, NO.2), KNOWN AS THE "TAX REFORM CODE OF 1971."
23     (h) (I)  As used in this section, unless the context           <--
24  otherwise requires:
25     "District election board" means election officers required
26  for the conduct of elections in any election district in

29     "Election" means any general, municipal, special or primary
30  election.
1     "Election district" means a district, division or precinct
2  established in accordance with the act of June 3, 1937
3  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the "Pennsylvania Election Code,"
4  in which all qualified electors are eligible to vote in the same
5  polling place.
6     Section 3.  (a)  Section 412.1 of the act of June 3, 1937
7  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, is
8  repealed.
9     (b)  All other acts and parts of acts are repealed insofar as
10  they are inconsistent with this act.
11     Section 4.  This act shall be applicable as constitutionally
12  permissible.
13     Section 5.  This act shall take effect January 1, 1998.
