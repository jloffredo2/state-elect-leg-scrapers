5  Section 1.  Short title.
6     This act shall be known and may be cited as the District
7  Election Officers Compensation Act.
8  Section 2.  Definitions.
9     The following words and phrases when used in this act shall
10  have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
11  context clearly indicates otherwise:
12     "District Election Board."  The election officers required
13  for the conduct of elections in any election district in
14  accordance with the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320),
15  known as the Pennsylvania Election Code.
16     "Election."  Any general, municipal, special or primary
1  election.
2     "Election district."  A district, division or precinct
3  established in accordance with the act of June 3, 1937
4  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, in
5  which all qualified electors vote in one polling place.
6  Section 3.  Compensation.
7     (a)  Schedule.--Compensation of judges of election,
8  inspectors of election, clerks and machine operators as provided
9  in Article IV of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320),
10  known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, shall be fixed by the
11  county board of elections for each election in accordance with
12  the following:
13        Election Officers         Minimum        Maximum
14                                Compensation   Compensation
15      Judges of elections           $45            $100
16      Inspectors of election,
17          clerks and machine
18          operators                 $40            $ 95
19     (b)  Per diem by category.--The county board of elections
20  may, in its discretion, establish different per diem rates
21  within the above-mentioned minima and maxima based on the number
22  of votes cast for the following groups:
23         (1)  150 votes or less.
24         (2)  151 to 300 votes.
25         (3)  301 to 500 votes.
26         (4)  501 to 750 votes.
27         (5)  751 votes and over.
28  Section 4.  Additional compensation.
29     (a)  Transmitting returns and ballot boxes.--For transmitting
30  returns of elections and the ballot box or boxes, all judges of
1  elections shall be entitled to receive the additional sum of
2  $10.
3     (b)  Minority inspector.--The county board of elections may,
4  in its discretion, require the minority inspector of elections
5  to accompany the judge of elections in transmitting the returns
6  of elections in which case the minority inspector of elections
7  shall be entitled to receive the additional sum of $10.
8     (c)  Mileage.--The person furnishing transportation to the
9  judge of elections and the minority inspector in transmitting
10  returns and ballot boxes shall be entitled to a minimum of 30Â¢
11  per circular mile from the polling place to the county court
12  house. The name of such person shall appear on the voucher of
13  the judge of elections and only one person shall receive mileage
14  compensation.
15     (d)  Constable or deputy constable.--A constable or deputy
16  constable performing duties under section 1207 of the act of
17  June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania
18  Election Code, shall receive compensation at the same rate
19  payable to an inspector or a clerk under section 3.
20  Section 5.  Multiple elections.
21     When a primary and special election or a special election and
22  a general or municipal election take place on the same date,
23  they shall be construed as one election for the purpose of
24  receiving compensation.
25  Section 6.  Repeals.
26     (a)  Absolute.--Section 412.1 of the act of June 3, 1937
27  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, is
28  repealed.
29     (b)  General.--All other acts and parts of acts are repealed
30  insofar as they are inconsistent with this act.
