16     Section 1.  Section 401 of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333,
17  No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, amended
18  February 10, 1956 (P.L.1019, No.319), is amended to read:
19     Section 401.  District Election Boards; Election.--(a)  All
20  primaries and elections shall be conducted in each election
21  district by a district election board consisting of a judge of
22  election, a majority inspector of election and a minority
1  inspector of election, assisted by clerks and machine inspectors
2  in certain cases, as hereinafter provided.
3     (b)  Persons qualified to serve as and seeking election to
4  the office of judge of election, majority inspector of election
5  and minority inspector of election may have their names placed
6  upon the primary ballots by filing a declaration of candidacy
7  with the county board. The Secretary of the Commonwealth shall
8  prescribe the format of the declaration of candidacy. The
9  declaration of candidacy shall include a candidate affidavit.
10     (c)  The judge and inspectors of election of each election
11  district shall be elected by the electors thereof at the
12  municipal election, and shall hold office for a term of four
13  years from the first Monday of January next succeeding their
14  election.
15     (d)  Each elector may vote for one person as judge and for
16  one person as inspector, and the person receiving the highest
17  number of votes for judge shall be declared elected judge of
18  election, the person receiving the highest number of votes for
19  inspector shall be declared elected majority inspector of
20  election, and the person receiving the second highest number of
21  votes for inspector shall be declared elected minority inspector
22  of election.
