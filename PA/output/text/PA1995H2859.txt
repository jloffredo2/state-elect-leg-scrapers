11     Section 1.  Section 102 of the act of June 30, 1995 (P.L.170,
12  No.25), known as the Pennsylvania Voter Registration Act, is
13  amended by adding a definition to read:
14  Section 102.  Definitions.
15     The following words and phrases when used in this act shall
16  have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
17  context clearly indicates otherwise:
18     * * *
19     "Qualifying educational institution."  Any of the following:
1         (1)  A university which is a member of the State System
2     of Higher Education.
3         (2)  A State-related university.
4         (3)  A community college.
5         (4)  A private college or university.
6     * * *
7     Section 2.  Sections 521, 525, 526, 527(c) and (d) and 5102
8  of the act are amended to read:
9  Section 521.  Methods of voter registration.
10     An individual qualified to register to vote under section
11  501(a) may apply to register as follows:
12         (1)  In person under section 522.
13         (2)  In conjunction with an application for a motor
14     vehicle driver's license under section 523.
15         (3)  By mail application under section 524.
16         (4)  In conjunction with certain government agency or
17     educational services under section 525.
18  Section 525.  Government agencies and affiliated entities.
19     (a)  General rule.--The secretary shall administer a system
20  whereby all offices in this Commonwealth that provide public
21  assistance, each county clerk of Orphan's Court, including each
22  marriage license bureau, all offices in this Commonwealth that
23  provide State-funded programs primarily engaged in providing
24  services to persons with disabilities, all qualifying
25  educational institutions and all armed forces recruitment
26  centers do all of the following:
27         (1)  Distribute voter registration applications with each
28     application; reapplication; and application for
29     recertification, renewal or change of address. A qualifying
30     educational institution shall make the distribution under
1     this paragraph at registration, issuance of a student
2     identification number, application for financial aid, payment
3     of tuition and student orientation and at other appropriate
4     times.
5         (2)  Assist applicants with completion of the
6     registration application unless assistance is refused.
7         (3)  Accept completed registration applications.
8         (4)  Transmit completed applications to the appropriate
9     registration commission.
10     (b)  Forms.--An agency or qualifying educational institution
11  designated in subsection (a) shall provide a form for office
12  visits or, if the agency provides services to persons with
13  disabilities, for home visits which contains all of the
14  following:
15         (1)  The question, "If you are not registered to vote
16     where you live now, would you like to apply to register to
17     vote today?"
18         (2)  If [the] an agency provides public assistance, the
19     statement, "Applying to register or declining to register to
20     vote will not affect the amount of assistance that you will
21     be provided by this agency."
22         (3)  Boxes for the applicant to check to indicate whether
23     the applicant would like to register or decline to register
24     to vote. In close proximity to the boxes the following words
25     shall appear in prominent type: "IF YOU DO NOT CHECK EITHER
26     BOX, YOU WILL BE CONSIDERED TO HAVE DECIDED NOT TO REGISTER
27     TO VOTE AT THIS TIME."
28         (4)  The statement, "In order to be qualified to register
29     to vote, you must be at least 18 years of age on the day of
30     the next election, you must have been a citizen of the United
1     States for at least one month prior to the next election and
2     have resided in Pennsylvania and the election district where
3     you plan to vote for at least 30 days prior to the next
4     election, and you must not have been confined to a penal
5     institution for a conviction of a felony within the last five
6     years."
7         (5)  The statement, "If you would like help in filling
8     out the voter registration application form, we will help
9     you. The decision whether to seek help is yours. You may fill
10     out the form in private."
11         (6)  The statement, "If you believe that someone has
12     interfered with your right to register or to decline to
13     register to vote, your right to privacy in deciding whether
14     to register or in applying to register to vote or your right
15     to choose your own political party or other political
16     preference, you may file a complaint with the Secretary of
17     the Commonwealth, Pennsylvania Department of State,
18     Harrisburg, PA 17120." The secretary shall establish and
19     publish a toll-free telephone number for the purpose of
20     receiving complaints.
21     (c)  Effect.--Failure to check either box under subsection
22  (b)(3) shall be considered a declination to register to vote.
23     (d)  Staff.--[Agency employees] Employees assisting in the
24  completion of voter registration applications shall conduct
25  themselves in a manner consistent with the following principles:
26         (1)  They shall not seek to influence an applicant's
27     political preference or party registration or display
28     political preference or party allegiance.
29         (2)  They shall not make any statement to an applicant or
30     take any action the purpose of or effect of which is to
1     discourage the applicant from registering to vote.
2         (3)  They shall not make any statement to an applicant or
3     take any action the purpose or effect of which is to lead the
4     applicant to believe that a decision to register or not to
5     register has any bearing on the availability of services or
6     benefits.
7  Agency employees who violate this subsection shall be removed
8  from employment, provided that the agency at its discretion may
9  impose a penalty of suspension without pay for at least 30 days,
10  but not more than 120 days, if it finds that the violation does
11  not warrant termination.
12     (e)  Encouraging registration.--An agency or qualifying
13  educational institution designated in subsection (a) shall
14  provide reasonable space for nonpartisan signs or posters
15  encouraging voter registration. The signs and posters shall be
16  provided by the secretary.
17     (f)  Transmission.--An agency or qualifying educational
18  institution designated in subsection (a) shall forward all
19  completed registration applications to the appropriate
20  registration commission within ten days after the date of
21  receipt. If a voter registration application is received within
22  five days before the last day to register before an election,
23  the application shall be transmitted to the appropriate
24  commission not later than five days after the date of its
25  receipt by the agency or qualifying educational institution.
26     (g)  Confidentiality.--The identity of the voter registration
27  agency through which any particular voter is registered in
28  accordance with this section shall not be disclosed to the
29  public.
30     (h)  Use of information.--No information relating to a
1  declination to register to vote in connection with an
2  application made at an office described in this section may be
3  used for any purpose other than voter registration.
4     (i)  Assistance.--Each agency or qualifying educational
5  institution shall provide to each applicant who chooses to
6  register to vote the same degree of assistance with regard to
7  the completion of the registration application form as is
8  provided by the office with regard to the completion of its own
9  forms, unless the applicant refuses such assistance.
10     (j)  Regulation.--The secretary shall promulgate regulations
11  regarding the maintenance and destruction of forms used pursuant
12  to this section.
13  Section 526.  Time.
14     (a)  General rule.--Except as provided in subsection (b),
15  each registration commission, commissioners and registrars or
16  clerks appointed by the commission shall receive, during
17  ordinary business hours and during additional hours as the
18  commission prescribes, at the office of the commission and at
19  additional places as the commission designates, applications
20  from individuals who appear and claim that they are entitled to
21  be registered as electors of a municipality.
22     (b)  Deadlines.--In the administration of voter registration,
23  each commission shall ensure that any eligible applicant is
24  registered to vote in an election when the applicant has met any
25  of the following conditions:
26         (1)  In the case of voter registration with a motor
27     vehicle driver's license application under section 523, if
28     the valid voter registration application is submitted to the
29     appropriate Department of Transportation office not later
30     than 30 days before the date of the election.
1         (2)  In the case of registration by mail under section
2     524, if the valid voter registration mail application is
3     postmarked not later than the deadline to register for the
4     ensuing election or, in the case of an illegible or missing
5     postmark, it is received not later than five days after the
6     deadline to register for the ensuing election.
7         (3)  In the case of voter registration [at a voter
8     registration agency] under section 525, if the valid voter
9     registration application is accepted at the voter
10     registration agency or qualifying educational institution not
11     later than 30 days before the date of the election.
12         (4)  In any other case, if the valid voter registration
13     application of the applicant is received by the appropriate
14     commission not later than 30 days before the election.
15     (c)  Special rules.--
16         (1)  In the case of a special election within a
17     congressional, senatorial or representative district held on
18     a day other than the day of a primary, general or municipal
19     election, the registration application forms shall not be
20     processed in the wards and election districts comprising the
21     district for the 30 days prior to the special election for
22     such election.
23         (2)  No applications shall be received as follows:
24             (i)  On Sundays.
25             (ii)  On holidays.
26             (iii)  On the day of the election.
27             (iv)  During the 30 days next preceding each
28         general, municipal and primary election, except as
29         provided in subsection (b).

3  Section 5102.  Application for absentee ballots.
4     (a)  General rule.--Notwithstanding the provisions of this
5  act or the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the
6  Pennsylvania Election Code, the following persons may make
7  application for an absentee ballot by sending a letter or other
8  signed document to the county board of elections in the county
9  in which the person's voting address is located:
10         (1)  Any qualified elector who is unable to attend his
11     polling place on the day of any primary or election because
12     of illness or physical disability.
13         (2)  Any qualified elector who expects to be absent from
14     this Commonwealth or the county of residence because duties,
15     occupation or business require him to be elsewhere the day of
16     any primary or election.
17         (3)  A county employee who cannot vote due to duties
18     relating to the conduct of elections.
19         (4)  A person who will not attend a polling place because
20     of an observance of a religious holiday.
21     (b)  Contents of letter or document.--The letter or document
22  under subsection (a) shall provide the same information as is
23  provided on forms prescribed by the secretary.
24     (c)  Review and processing.--The letter or document shall be
25  subject to the same schedule as other applications for absentee
26  ballots and upon receipt by the county board of elections shall
27  be reviewed and processed in the same fashion as other
28  applications for absentee ballots.
29     (d)  Application prepared by political party.--An absentee
30  ballot application form containing the same information as that
1  contained on the form prescribed by the secretary, which was
2  prepared or distributed by a political party and signed by a
3  qualified elector, shall be deemed for all purposes as valid and
4  shall be reviewed and processed by the county board of elections
5  in the same manner as applications on forms prescribed by the
6  secretary and supplied by the county board of elections.
7     (e)  Qualifying educational institutions.--Qualifying
8  educational institutions shall distribute absentee ballot
9  application forms at conspicuous locations. A form under this
10  subsection must contain the same information as that contained
11  on the form prescribed by the secretary. A form under this
12  subsection signed by a qualified elector shall be deemed for all
13  purposes as valid and shall be reviewed and processed by the
14  county board of elections in the same manner as applications on
15  forms prescribed by the secretary and supplied by the county
16  board of elections.
17     Section 3.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
