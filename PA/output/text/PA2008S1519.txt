15     Section 1.  The definition of "electronic voting system" in
16  section 1101-A of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320),
17  known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, added July 11, 1980
18  (P.L.600, No.128), is amended to read:
19     Section 1101-A.  Definitions.--As used in this article:
20     * * *
21     "Electronic voting system" means a system in which one or
22  more voting devices are used to permit the registering or
1  recording of votes and in which such votes are computed and
2  tabulated by automatic tabulating equipment. The system shall
3  provide for a permanent physical record of each vote cast. This
4  term shall include a paper-ballot-based voting system
5  established under section 1108.1-A.
6     * * *
7     Section 2.  The act is amended by adding a section to read:
8     Section 1108.1-A.  Commonwealth Funding.--(a)  The
9  Commonwealth shall provide funds necessary to those counties who
10  elect to convert to a paper-ballot-based voting system. For the
11  purposes of this section, a paper-ballot-based voting system is
12  one that uses a paper ballot marked by the voter by hand or a
13  paper ballot marked by the voter with the assistance of a
14  nontabulating ballot marking device. The Department of State
15  shall pay to each eligible county an amount equal to the sum of
16  the following:
17     (1)  Documented reasonable costs paid or incurred by a county
18  board of elections to replace any voting system used to conduct
19  elections that did not use a paper ballot marked by the voter by
20  hand or a paper ballot marked by the voter with the assistance
21  of a nontabulating ballot marking device.
22     (2)  The documented reasonable costs paid or incurred by a
23  county board of elections to obtain nontabulating ballot marking
24  devices that are accessible for individuals with disabilities in
25  accordance with the requirements of section 301(a)(3) of the
26  Help America Vote Act of 2002 (Public Law 107-252, 116 Stat.
27  1704).
28     (3)  The documented reasonable costs paid or incurred by a
29  county board of elections to obtain ballot marking stations or
30  voting booths for the protection of voter privacy.
1     (4)  The documented reasonable costs paid or incurred by a
2  county board of elections to obtain paper ballots.
3     (5)  The documented reasonable costs paid or incurred by a
4  county board of elections to obtain precinct-based equipment
5  that scans and tabulates paper ballots.
6     (6)  The documented reasonable administrative costs paid or
7  incurred by a county board of elections that are associated with
8  meeting the requirements of this act.
9     (7)  The determination of whether costs paid or incurred by a
10  county are reasonable shall be made by the Department of State.
11     (b)  In order to receive funding, the county must submit the
12  following information to the Department of State indicating
13  that:
14     (1)  In the last regularly scheduled election the voting
15  system that was used was not a paper-ballot-based voting system.
16     (2)  Upon the next election only paper-ballot voting systems
17  will be used.
18     (3)  A nontabulating ballot marking device will be available
19  and accessible for individuals with disabilities in each polling
20  place in accordance with the requirements of section 301(a)(3)
21  of the Help America Vote Act of 2002.
22     (4)  A sufficient number of ballot making stations or voting
23  booths will be available for the protection of voter privacy.
24     (5)  A sufficient number of paper ballots will be available.
25     (6)  Any other such information that may be required by the
26  Department of State to verify compliance with this act.
27     Section 3.  This act shall take effect immediately.
