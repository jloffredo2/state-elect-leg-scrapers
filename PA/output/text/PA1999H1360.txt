8     Section 1.  Section 903 of the act of June 30, 1995 (P.L.170,
9  No.25), known as the Pennsylvania Voter Registration Act, is
10  amended to read:
11  Section 903.  Change of enrollment of political party.
12     (a)  General rule.--By the deadline for registration for the
13  primary election, a registered voter who desires to change the
14  enrollment of political designation or who, although registered,
15  has not previously enrolled as a member of a party may appear
16  before a commissioner, registrar or clerk or may submit an
17  application by mail under section 524 and state in a signed
18  writing the political party in which the voter desires to be
19  enrolled. If the signature of the elector is verified by
1  comparison with the elector's signature as it appears on file
2  with the commission, the commissioner, registrar or clerk shall
3  make the change in the general register and district register.
4  If supported by other evidence of identity, a mark may be made
5  in lieu of a signature by an elector who is unable to write. The
6  mark must be made in the presence of a witness who must sign the
7  registration application.
8     (b)  Exception.--No registered voter may change his or her
9  political party or political body enrollment during the period
10  of time beginning with the day immediately following the
11  deadline for registration for the primary election and extending
12  through the general or municipal election of that same year.
13     Section 2.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
