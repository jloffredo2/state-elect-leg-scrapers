6     Section 2.  Section 910 of the act, amended February 19, 1986
7  (P.L.29, No.11), is amended to read:
8     Section 910.  Affidavits of Candidates.--Each candidate for
9  any State, county, city, borough, incorporated town, township,
10  ward, school district, poor district, election district, party
11  office, party delegate or alternate, or for the office of United
12  States Senator or Representative in Congress, shall file with
13  his nomination petition his affidavit stating--(a) his
14  residence, with street and number, if any, and his post-office
15  address; (b) his election district, giving city, borough, town
16  or township; (c) the name of the office for which he consents to
17  be a candidate; (d) that he is eligible for such office; (e)
18  that he will not knowingly violate any provision of this act, or
19  of any law regulating and limiting nomination and election
20  expenses and prohibiting corrupt practices in connection
21  therewith; (f) unless he is a candidate for judge of a court of
22  common pleas, the Philadelphia Municipal Court or the Traffic
23  Court of Philadelphia, or for the office of school director in a
24  district where that office is elective or for the office of
25  justice of the peace that he is not a candidate for nomination
26  for the same office of any party other than the one designated
27  in such petition; (g) if he is a candidate for a delegate, or
28  alternate delegate, member of State committee, National
29  committee or party officer, that he is a registered and enrolled
30  member of the designated party; (h) if he is a candidate for
1  delegate or alternate delegate the presidential candidate to
2  whom he is committed or the term "uncommitted"; and (i) that he
3  is aware of the provisions of section 1626 of this act requiring
4  pre-election and post-election reporting of campaign
5  contributions and expenditures. In cases of petitions for
6  candidates for the General Assembly, the candidate's affidavit
7  shall state (1) that the candidate will satisfy the eligibility
8  requirements contained in sections 5 and 7 of Article II of the
9  Constitution of Pennsylvania; (2) (i) that in the case of a
10  candidate for the office of Senator in the General Assembly that
11  the candidate will be twenty-five (25) years of age on or before
12  the first day of the term for which the candidate seeks election
13  or (ii) that in the case of a candidate for the office of
14  Representative in the General Assembly that the candidate will
15  be twenty-one (21) years of age on or before the first day of
16  the term for which the candidate seeks election; (3) that the
17  candidate shall have been a citizen and inhabitant of
18  Pennsylvania four (4) years and an inhabitant of the respective
19  district one (1) year next before the election (unless absent on
20  the public business of the United States or of this State); and
21  (4) that the candidate has not been convicted of embezzlement of
22  public moneys, bribery, perjury or other infamous crime. In
23  cases of petitions for delegate and alternate delegate to
24  National conventions, the candidate's affidavit shall state that
25  his signature to the delegate's statement, as hereinafter set
26  forth, if such statement is signed by said candidate, was
27  affixed to the sheet or sheets of said petition prior to the
28  circulation of same. In the case of a candidate for nomination
29  as President of the United States, it shall not be necessary for
30  such candidate to file the affidavit required in this section to
1  be filed by candidates, but the post-office address of such
2  candidate shall be stated in such nomination petition. In the
3  case of a candidate for school director, the candidate's
4  affidavit shall state that he is not a candidate for nomination
5  for the same office for any term other than the one designated
6  in the petition.
7     Section 3.  The act is amended by adding a section to read:
8     Section 1210.1.  Procedure for Certain Electors.--(a)  If an
9  elector believes himself or herself to be registered and shows
10  the judge of election proof of residency in that election
11  district by displaying a valid driver's license or any other
12  document approved by the Department of State, but his or her
13  registration card and affidavit does not appear in the district
14  register in the election district in which he or she resides on
15  election day, the judge of election shall make a good faith
16  attempt to verify the registration or ascertain if the elector
17  resides or is properly registered in another election district.
18  If the judge of election is unable to verify the registration,
19  the elector, notwithstanding the provisions of section 1210(d),
20  may fill out a standardized form comprised of a registration
21  card and affidavit, along with a paper ballot. The judge of
22  election upon completion of voting activity shall forward this
23  standardized form to the county board for verification of voter
24  registration. If voter registration in the election district in
25  which he or she resides is verified, the county board shall
26  canvass the ballot.
27     (b)  If the voter registration in the election district in
28  which the elector resides is not verified, the county board
29  shall register him or her as an elector in accordance with law,
30  but the elector's vote shall not be counted in the instant
1  election.
2     (c)  The Department of State shall provide standardized forms
3  and promulgate regulations to carry out subsections (a) and (b)
4  of this section.
5     Section 4.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
