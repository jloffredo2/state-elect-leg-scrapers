14  February 13, 1998 (P.L.72, No.18), are amended to read:
15     Section 1302.  Applications for Official Absentee Ballots.--*
16  * *
17     (f)  The county chairman of each political party or the head
18  of each political body shall designate one representative from
19  his respective political party or body for each public or
20  private institution. The representatives so appointed shall, at
21  the same time on a date fixed by the county board of election
22  visit every public or private institution situate in the county
23  for the purpose of obtaining the names and addresses of public
24  or private institution residents who desire to receive
25  applications for absentee ballots and to act as an election
26  board as provided in subsection (g) of this section. The list of
27  names and addresses thus obtained shall then be submitted by
28  said representatives to the board which shall furnish
29  applications individually to those appearing in the written
30  request. If the chairman or head of a political party or body
1  fails to appoint a representative within fifteen days from
2  written notice from the county board of election, the county
3  board of election shall appoint a representative from the
4  political party or body.
5     (g)  The county board of election shall appoint teams of
6  three members for each public or private institution that shall
7  go to the public or private institutions and hold the election
8  on the first Friday prior to election day. Each member of the
9  board shall appoint one member on every team. After the votes
10  are cast, the teams shall collect the ballots and return them to
11  the county board of election where they shall be placed unopened
12  in a secure, safe and sealed container in the custody of the
13  board until they shall be distributed to the respective absentee
14  voters' election district as provided in section 1308 of this
15  act where they shall be counted with the other absentee ballots,
16  if any.
