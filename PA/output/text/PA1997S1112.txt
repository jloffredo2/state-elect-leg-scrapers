5  Section 1.  Short title.
6     This act shall be known and may be cited as the District
7  Election Officers Compensation Act.
8  Section 2.  Definitions.
9     The following words and phrases when used in this act shall
10  have the meanings given to them in this section unless the
11  context clearly indicates otherwise:
12     "District Election Board."  The election officers required
13  for the conduct of elections in any election district in
14  accordance with the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320),
15  known as the Pennsylvania Election Code.
16     "Election."  Any general, municipal, special or primary
17  election.
1     "Election district."  A district, division or precinct
2  established in accordance with the act of June 3, 1937
3  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, in
4  which all qualified electors vote in one polling place.
5  Section 3.  Compensation.
6     (a)  Schedule.--Compensation of judges of election,
7  inspectors of election, clerks and machine operators as provided
8  in Article IV of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320),
9  known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, shall be fixed by the
10  county board of elections for each election in accordance with
11  the following:
12           Election Officers              Minimum       Maximum
13                                       Compensation   Compensation
14      Judges of elections                $45.00          $100.00
15      Inspectors of Election,
16      clerks and machine operators       $40.00          $95.00
17     (b)  Per diem by category.--The county board of elections
18  may, in its discretion, establish different per diem rates
19  within the above-mentioned minima and maxima based on the number
20  of votes cast for the following groups:
21         (1)  150 votes or less.
22         (2)  151 to 300 votes.
23         (3)  301 to 500 votes.
24         (4)  501 to 750 votes.
25         (5)  751 votes and over.
26  Section 4.  Additional compensation.
27     (a)  Transmitting returns and ballot boxes.--For transmitting
28  returns of elections and the ballot box or boxes, all judges of
29  elections shall be entitled to receive the additional sum of
30  $10.
1     (b)  Minority inspector.--The county board of elections may,
2  in its discretion, require the minority inspector of elections
3  to accompany the judge of elections in transmitting the returns
4  of elections in which case the minority inspector of elections
5  shall be entitled to receive the additional sum of $10.
6     (c)  Mileage.--The person furnishing transportation to the
7  judge of elections and the minority inspector in transmitting
8  returns and ballot boxes shall be entitled to a minimum of 30Â¢
9  per circular mile from the polling place to the county court
10  house. The name of such person shall appear on the voucher of
11  the judge of elections and only one person shall receive mileage
12  compensation.
13     (d)  Constable or deputy constable.--A constable or deputy
14  constable performing duties under section 1207 of the act of
15  June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania
16  Election Code, shall receive compensation at the same rate
17  payable to an inspector or a clerk under section 3.
18  Section 5.  Multiple elections.
19     When a primary and special election or a special election and
20  a general or municipal election take place on the same date,
21  they shall be construed as one election for the purpose of
22  receiving compensation.
23  Section 6.  Repeals.
24     (a)  Specific.--Section 412.1 of the act of June 3, 1937
25  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, is
26  repealed.
27     (b)  Inconsistent.--All acts and parts of acts are repealed
28  insofar as they are inconsistent with this act.
29  Section 7.  Application of act.
30     This act shall be applicable as constitutionally permissible.
1  Section 8.  Effective date.
2     This act shall take effect January 1, 1998.
