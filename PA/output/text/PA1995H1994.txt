15     Section 1.  Section 1218 of the act of June 3, 1937
16  (P.L.1333, No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code,
17  amended February 13, 1989 (P.L.1, No.1), is amended and the
18  section is amended by adding a subsection to read:
19     Section 1218.  Assistance in Voting.--
20     (a)  No voter shall be permitted to receive any assistance in
1  voting at any [primary or] election, unless there is recorded
2  upon [his] the voter's registration card [his] a declaration, or
3  unless the voter declares in writing at the polling place, that,
4  by reason of blindness, disability, or inability to read or
5  write, [he] the voter is unable to read the names on the ballot
6  or on the voting machine labels, or that [he] the voter has a
7  physical disability which renders [him] the voter unable to see
8  or mark the ballot or operate the voting machine, or to enter
9  the voting compartment or voting machine booth without
10  assistance, the exact nature of such condition being recorded on
11  such registration card or declaration, and unless the election
12  officers are satisfied that [he] the voter still suffers from
13  the same condition. The declaration completed in the polling
14  place shall be in a form approved by the Secretary of the
15  Commonwealth, substantially as follows:
16     I, (Name and address of elector requiring assistance) by
17     reason of (Reason for need of assistance) am unable to vote
18     without the assistance of (Name and address of person
19     rendering assistance).
20  Date
21  Witnessed by (Name of Witness)
22  (Signature of Judge of Election)
23     (b)  Any elector who is entitled to receive assistance in
24  voting under the provisions of this section shall be permitted
25  by the judge of election to select a person of the elector's
26  choice to enter the voting compartment or voting machine booth
27  with him to assist him in voting, such assistance to be rendered
28  inside the voting compartment or voting machine booth except
29  that the elector's employer or an agent of the employer or an
30  officer or agent of the elector's union shall not be eligible to
1  assist the elector.
2     (c)  In every case of assistance under the provisions of this
3  section, the judge of election shall forthwith enter in writing
4  in a book to be furnished by the county board of elections, to
5  be known as the record of assisted voters--(1) the voter's name;
6  (2) a statement of the facts which entitle him to receive
7  assistance; and (3) the name of the person furnishing the
8  assistance. The record of assisted voters shall be returned by
9  the judge of election to the county board of elections with the
10  other papers, as hereinafter provided, and said county board
11  shall permit the same to be examined only upon the written order
12  of a judge of the court of common pleas: Provided, however, That
13  such record shall be subject to subpoena to the same extent to
14  which other election records may be subpoenaed: And provided
15  further, That the county election board shall permit any
16  registration commission to examine any records of assisted
17  voters without a court order, in order that the registration
18  commission may ascertain whether electors, who have declared, at
19  the time of registration, their need for assistance, actually
20  did receive assistance when voting at any election.
21     (d)  Subsequent to the election, the county board of
22  elections shall record the facts of the declaration completed in
23  the polling place under subsection (a) onto the elector's
24  registration card, including the reason the elector requires
25  assistance.
26     Section 2.  Section 1302.1 of the act, amended December 11,
27  1968 (P.L.1183, No.375), is amended to read:
28     Section 1302.1.  Date of Application for Absentee Ballot.--
29     (a)  Applications for absentee ballots unless otherwise
30  specified shall be received in the office of the county board of
1  elections not earlier than fifty (50) days before the primary or
2  election and not later than five o'clock P.M. of the first
3  Tuesday prior to the day of any primary or election: Provided,
4  however, That in the event any elector otherwise qualified who
5  is so physically disabled or ill on or before the first Tuesday
6  prior to any primary or election that he is unable to file his
7  application or who becomes physically disabled or ill after the
8  first Tuesday prior to any primary or election and is unable to
9  appear at his polling place or any elector otherwise qualified
10  who because of the conduct of his business, duties or occupation
11  will necessarily be absent from the State or county of his
12  residence on the day of the primary or election, which fact was
13  not and could not reasonably be known to said elector on or
14  before the first Tuesday prior to any primary or election, shall
15  be entitled to an absentee ballot at any time prior to [five
16  o'clock P.M. on the first Friday] the close of business on the
17  first Monday immediately preceding any primary or election upon
18  execution of an Emergency Application in such form prescribed by
19  the Secretary of the Commonwealth.
20     (b)  In the case of an elector who is physically disabled or
21  ill on or before the first Tuesday prior to a primary or
22  election or becomes physically disabled or ill after the first
23  Tuesday prior to a primary or election, such Emergency
24  Application shall contain a supporting affidavit from his
25  attending physician stating that due to physical disability or
26  illness said elector was unable to apply for an absentee ballot
27  on or before the first Tuesday prior to the primary or election
28  or became physically disabled or ill after that period.
29     (c)  In the case of an elector who is necessarily absent
30  because of the conduct of his business, duties or occupation
1  under the unforeseen circumstances specified in this subsection,
2  such Emergency Application shall contain a supporting affidavit
3  from such elector stating that because of the conduct of his
4  business, duties or occupation said elector will necessarily be
5  absent from the State or county of his residence on the day of
6  the primary or election which fact was not and could not
7  reasonably be known to said elector on or before the first
8  Tuesday prior to the primary or election.
9     Section 3.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
