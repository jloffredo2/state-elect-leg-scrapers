14     Section 1.  Section 909 of the act of June 3, 1937 (P.L.1333,
15  No.320), known as the Pennsylvania Election Code, amended
16  February 19, 1986 (P.L.29, No.11), is amended to read:
17     Section 909.  Petition May Consist of Several Sheets;
18  Affidavit of Circulator.--Said nomination petition may be on one
19  or more sheets, and different sheets must be used for signers
20  resident in different counties. If more than one sheet is used,
21  they shall be bound together when offered for filing if they are
22  intended to constitute one petition, and each sheet shall be
1  numbered consecutively beginning with number one, at the foot of
2  each page. In cases of petitions for delegate or alternate
3  delegate to National conventions, each sheet shall contain a
4  notation indicating the presidential candidate to whom he is
5  committed or the term "uncommitted." Each sheet shall have
6  appended thereto the affidavit of the circulator of each sheet,
7  setting forth--(a) that he or she is a qualified elector duly
8  registered and enrolled as a member of the designated party of
9  the State, or of the political district, as the case may be,
10  referred to in said petition, unless said petition relates to
11  the nomination of a candidate for the office of school director
12  in a district where that office is elective, for a court of
13  common pleas, for the Philadelphia Municipal Court [or], for the
14  Traffic Court of Philadelphia or for [justice of the peace]
15  magisterial district judge, in which event the circulator need
16  not be a duly registered and enrolled member of the designated
17  party; (b) his residence, giving city, borough or township, with
18  street and number, if any; (c) that the signers thereto signed
19  with full knowledge of the contents of the petition; (d) that
20  their respective residences are correctly stated therein; (e)
21  that they all reside in the county named in the affidavit; (f)
22  that each signed on the date set opposite his name; and (g)
23  that, to the best of affiant's knowledge and belief, the signers
24  are qualified electors and duly registered and enrolled members
25  of the designated party of the State, or of the political
26  district, as the case may be.
27     Section 2.  This act shall take effect in 60 days.
